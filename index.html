Akinmedes' energy solutions, powered by years of research and novel piezoelectricity methods and technology, offer miniaturised, efficient, and flexible electrification on a global scale.

Delivering the world’s first Piezoelectric Generators.

Akinmedes energy solutions are built with Piezoelectric modules. Each module contains Ceramic Perovskite materials and proprietary arrangement and actuation methods that are significantly more smaller, efficient and resilient than traditional electromagnetic generators. This miniaturisation allows for great reduction in size and weight of generators and higher electromechanical coupling which produces more energy than traditional electromagnetic generators. Akinmedes’s first Piezoelectric Generators will be available in 5KW units, which can easily be combined with existing Power Management Systems.

Piezoelectricity is set to race Electromagnetism and Photovoltaics to a sustainable electric future.

Efficient

SiC SSTs are far more efficient than their traditional counterparts. These transformers are based on power electronics and use semiconductor devices to convert and control electrical power, leading to a compact design and efficient power conversion.

Intelligent

SiC SSTs can be digitally controlled and monitored, providing essential data for refined grid management. Their capability to manage real-time, bidirectional power flow is essential for optimizing the utilization of data centers, renewable energy, energy storage, and EV fast-charging deployments, as well as enabling monetized grid services.

Resilient

SiC SSTs can isolate loads from the connected grid to prevent faults. This capability enhances grid resilience in the event of disturbances and outages. They can also improve power quality, addressing challenges like voltage fluctuations and harmonic distortion, ensuring a stable and reliable energy supply.

Our Story

Our SST technology was pioneered by the Energy Research Institute at Singapore’s Nanyang Technological University (NTU) and spun out by Xora Innovation.

In 2022, the team demonstrated a 22KV 1.5MW SST system, and after six months of incubation work led by Xora Innovation, akinmedes was formed in May of 2023 with plans to commercialize NTU’s SST technology.
